With iris.

iris is a voice-first booking assistant paired with a high-contrast E-ink companion. It helps travelers with narrowed vision book flights independently without scanning fatigue.

Explore the concept Watch flow

Primary Persona

Julia, 53, hospital call center staff with glaucoma

Project Team

Team 2 - MD8215 CA1

Problem Statement

Does more information help us see better, or does it just get in the way?

Julia can only see narrow regions due to glaucoma. Dense airline interfaces force exhaustive scanning, increasing eye fatigue, stress, and fear of costly mistakes.

The Accessibility Gap

Singapore Airlines booking flows include low-contrast labels, compact airport rows, dense calendar grids, and subtle color cues that are difficult to parse in a 15-degree visual field.

WCAG issues found: 1.4.3, 1.4.11, 2.4.6, 2.5.8, 1.4.1

What Julia needs

  • Clear, high-contrast key information
  • Reduced scanning across wide layouts
  • Strong confirmation for high-stakes actions
  • A way to stay oriented at every step

Research Insights

Why current booking flows break down

25%

More saccadic eye movements are needed in glaucoma to piece together a visual scene, increasing effort and fatigue.

70%

Reported glare disability in glaucoma patients, where bright screens can wash out remaining vision.

40%

Reaction time can increase when processing low-contrast or complex visuals, raising cognitive load.

Problem Space 1

Improve perceivability of key information while reducing strain.

Problem Space 2

Communicate progress and options without extensive scanning.

Problem Space 3

Replace booking anxiety with confidence and error safety.

How might we recreate a booking experience for Julia, who has a narrowed field of vision, to book flights independently without extensive scanning fatigue?

Introducing iris

A booking system built for confidence, not overload

iris combines contextual voice guidance, persistent low-strain summaries, and deliberate interaction checkpoints to reduce cognitive friction and prevent high-cost errors.

ContextVoice

Curates A/B choices and relevant next steps instead of reading every UI element, so Julia can decide faster with less strain.

InkView

A high-contrast E-ink summary that mirrors confirmed choices, turning fleeting voice interactions into persistent anchors.

ProgressBar

Gives immediate orientation when Julia backtracks or edits choices, strengthening error management and trust.

MagSnap

A snap-on hardware control where the physical button is reserved for high-stakes actions like payment confirmation.

FocusFrame

Future calibration mode that scales interface framing to the user's visible window, supporting long-term independence as vision narrows.

Interaction Flow

How iris guides a booking session

  1. Destination and dates

    Julia says her route and dates. The system confirms and stores the summary.

  2. Compare options

    ContextVoice explains trade-offs one by one: lower price vs shorter layover.

  3. Confirm assistance and seat

    Special assistance and seat requests are repeated clearly before confirmation.

  4. Finalize with control

    Payment only proceeds after explicit final confirmation and persistent summary review.

Outcome

From exhaustion to empowerment

Before

40+ mins

Long scanning on dense booking screens caused fatigue and fear of irreversible mistakes.

After

80% shorter

Shorter sessions by reducing saccadic stress and search overhead, with clearer checkpoints and lower uncertainty.
